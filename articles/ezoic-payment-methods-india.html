Ezoic Payment Methods in India: Wise vs Payoneer vs Direct Bank Wire

By Ashish Divakar • Reading Time: 7 mins • Published: July 12, 2026

If you've recently been accepted into the Ezoic ad network or Ezoic Access Now program in India, congratulations! You're on your way to earning passive ad income from your website traffic. However, when it comes to getting paid, selecting the right payout method is crucial to avoid losing a percentage of your hard-earned money to hidden transfer fees and poor currency conversion rates.

In this guide, we break down the best Ezoic payment methods for Indian publishers, compare their transaction fees, and walk you through how to configure your account for direct INR bank deposits.

Overview of Ezoic Payment Options in India

Ezoic pays publishers on a net-30 schedule (usually between the 27th and 31st of each month) for earnings accumulated during the previous month. The minimum payout threshold is $20. As a publisher in India, you have three primary payment channels:

The Hidden Costs: Conversion Math for a $1,000 Payout

Many publishers select PayPal or Bank Wire thinking the transaction fees are flat and simple. In reality, the exchange rate spread is where you lose the most money. Let's calculate exactly how much money lands in your Indian bank account for a **$1,000 USD payout** under a mid-market rate of **1 USD = 83.50 INR**:

1. Why Payoneer is the Recommended Choice for Beginners

For most Indian publishers, Payoneer represents the perfect balance of convenience and low cost. Payoneer integrates directly into the Ezoic payment portal, which means there are zero manual wire transfer fees. When Ezoic sends your USD payout, Payoneer automatically converts it to Indian Rupees (INR) and transfers it directly to your linked Indian bank account (via local NEFT/IMPS transfer) within 24 hours.

Additionally, the minimum payout threshold is only $20, meaning you don't have to wait until you accumulate $100 to receive your money.

2. Wise: The Best Exchange Rates for High Earners

If you have high monthly traffic and are earning more than $500/month, Wise is an excellent alternative. Wise offers the actual mid-market exchange rate (the one you see on Google) and charges a transparent, low fee. To use Wise with Ezoic, you must obtain a virtual USD receiving account from Wise and enter those details as a standard bank account inside the Ezoic portal.

How to Configure Payoneer in Your Ezoic Account

Step-by-Step Payoneer Setup:

  1. Log in to your Ezoic Dashboard.
  2. Navigate to the Account settings page and click on Payment Settings.
  3. Under Payment Method, select Payoneer.
  4. Click the link to sign up or connect an existing Payoneer account.
  5. During setup, select an individual account and link your local Indian bank account details (including IFSC code).
  6. Select the correct Purpose Code (use P1007 - Advertising and Market Research) to ensure compliance with RBI regulations.

How to Configure Wise (ACH Transfer) in Your Ezoic Account

Step-by-Step Wise Setup:

  1. Log in to your Wise Account, navigate to your USD balance, and click on **Receiving Account Details**.
  2. Copy your **Routing Number**, **Account Number**, and **Account Type** (usually Checking).
  3. Log in to your **Ezoic Dashboard** and navigate to **Payment Settings**.
  4. Select **Direct Deposit (ACH/Wire)** as your payment method.
  5. Input your virtual USD routing and account details. Save settings. Ezoic will now pay Wise via free local ACH transfers, and Wise will transfer the funds to your Indian bank account at the mid-market rate.

What is the RBI Purpose Code & Why It Matters

When receiving foreign funds in India, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) requires a purpose code to identify the nature of the transaction. For ad revenue payouts from Ezoic or AdSense, you should select: P1007 (Advertising, market research and public opinion polling services). Choosing this code prevents your local bank from placing a temporary hold on your incoming transfer.

GST & FIRC Compliance for Indian Bloggers

Under Indian tax law, ad revenue received in foreign currency is considered a **Zero-Rated Export of Services**. To prove to tax authorities that your earnings are export services (and therefore exempt from the 18% GST), you must obtain a **Foreign Inward Remittance Certificate (FIRC)**. Here is how to retrieve it:

  • On Payoneer: Go to **Activity > Documents** in your dashboard, find the transaction, and download the digital FIRC for free.
  • On Wise: Go to the transfer page, click on **Receipt**, and download the FIRC document. Keep these receipts saved for your tax filings.
