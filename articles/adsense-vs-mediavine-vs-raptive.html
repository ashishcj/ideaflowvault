AdSense vs Mediavine vs Raptive: Which Network Pays the Most?

📅 July 15, 2026 ✍️ Ashish Divakar ⏱️ 7 min read

For independent publishers, reaching critical traffic milestones is a cause for celebration. But it also presents a major business pivot: **when should you migrate away from beginner ad setups to premium management networks?**

While beginners start with Google AdSense on day one, growing sites eventually unlock premium platforms like Mediavine and Raptive (formerly known as AdThrive). The payout jump between these networks is massive—often tripling or quadrupling a website's monthly ad profit for the same number of pageviews.

In this guide, we will compare AdSense, Mediavine, and Raptive side-by-side. We'll look at the exact traffic requirements, average RPM yields, contract terms, page speed impacts, and help you determine when and how to apply.

2. Google AdSense: The Starter Tier

AdSense is the default starting point. With no minimum traffic limits, it allows any new blog or hobby site to begin monetizing immediately upon approval.

  • Pros: Zero entry threshold; instant integration; code works on any HTML site structure.
  • Cons: Very low RPM (usually $2-8 per 1,000 views). Manual optimization or basic Auto Ads are notorious for placing ads in awkward positions that ruin the user experience.
  • Best For: Brand new niche sites under 10,000 monthly pageviews.

3. Mediavine: The Growth Champion

Mediavine is widely loved in the blogging community, especially in lifestyle, travel, food, and home decor niches. To apply, your site must have at least **50,000 monthly sessions** (tracked via Google Analytics 4) and a clean track record with Google AdSense.

  • Pros: High-yield programmatic ads (typically $15-35+ RPM); top-tier customer support; incredibly lightweight scripts optimized for Core Web Vitals. Their dashboard gives you detailed page-by-page breakdown stats.
  • Cons: Long payment terms (Net-65 by default). Hard to get accepted if your traffic originates mostly outside Tier-1 English-speaking countries.
  • Best For: Mid-sized blogs seeking high revenue optimizations without hurting site speed.

4. Raptive: The Enterprise Leader

Raptive (formerly AdThrive) is the premier ad management network for large publishers. The application minimum stands at **100,000 monthly pageviews**, with at least 50% of your audience originating from the United States.

  • Pros: Maximized ad monetization rates ($20-50+ RPM); dedicated revenue specialists who manually set up layouts for your specific pages; premium video players that pay high rates.
  • Cons: High traffic barrier; strict application review process (requires 100% original, safe content and zero copyright issues).
  • Best For: High-traffic authority websites looking for custom enterprise-grade ad setups.

Why is the Revenue Gap So Large?

If you get 100,000 pageviews, your monthly earnings will vary drastically across these networks:

  • Google AdSense: 100,000 views @ $5 RPM = $500/month
  • Mediavine: 100,000 views @ $25 RPM = $2,500/month
  • Raptive: 100,000 views @ $35 RPM = $3,500/month

This huge discrepancy exists because premium networks sell your space through private marketplace deals (PMPs) directly to big brands. In contrast, AdSense relies entirely on open auctions, where rates are lower. Additionally, Mediavine and Raptive enforce ad density rules and scroll-refresh technology to guarantee high impressions without annoying visitors.
